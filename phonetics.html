Word Stress & Sentence Stress

Word Stress

Every English word with 2+ syllables has one stressed syllable (louder, longer, clearer).

  • 'photograph (1st syllable) → pho-to-graph
  • pho'tographer (2nd syllable) → pho-tog-ra-pher
  • photo'graphic (3rd syllable) → pho-to-graph-ic

Common Stress Patterns

  • 2-syllable nouns/adjectives: usually stress on 1st → 'doctor, 'happy, 'mother
  • 2-syllable verbs/prepositions: usually stress on 2nd → de'cide, ar'rive, be'tween
  • Words ending in -tion, -sion, -cian: stress on syllable before → educ'ation, deci'sion
  • Words ending in -ic, -ical: stress on syllable before → fan'tastic, eco'nomic
  • Compound nouns: stress on first part → 'bookshop, 'football, 'classroom

Sentence Stress

In a sentence, content words (nouns, verbs, adjectives, adverbs) are stressed. Function words (a, the, in, of, and) are usually unstressed.

"Ravi went to the shop to buy bread."
