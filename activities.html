Home · Activities

Classroom Activities

You don't get fluent by listening. Every week, every student does a mix of these activities — and that's what builds real confidence.

Group Discussions

Real GD topics every week. Learn to open, contribute, and conclude. Practice listening as much as speaking.

Debates

For/against arguments, rebuttals, time-bound speaking. Builds quick thinking and structure.

Public Speaking

Stage practice. Voice modulation, audience eye contact, and the discipline of speaking from notes.

Mock Interviews

HR rounds, technical-style questions, body language coaching, and feedback after every round.

Role Plays

Real scenarios — shopping, asking for directions, college viva, customer support — practiced in pairs.

Storytelling

Narrate, describe, and improvise. The fastest way to build natural fluency and vocabulary in context.

Word Games

Vocabulary games, idiom challenges, and tongue twisters — learning that doesn't feel like learning.

Daily News Reading

Read a short news item aloud, summarise it, and answer questions. Pronunciation and comprehension together.

Speaking Competitions

Periodic in-house contests. Best speakers, best debaters — a healthy push to perform.
