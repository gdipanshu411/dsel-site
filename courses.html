Teaching Style

How we actually teach

The DSEL classroom is built on three things — daily speaking, friendly correction, and real-life practice. Here's what that looks like.

Speak first, fix later

Students start speaking from day one. Mistakes are gently corrected without breaking the flow. Confidence comes before grammar perfection.

Real-life situations

Topics taught around real moments — meeting people, asking directions, attending interviews — not textbook scenarios.

Repetition with feedback

Same skills practiced multiple ways across the week. One-on-one feedback after every test or activity.

Group + individual time

Group activities for energy and momentum, individual sessions for personalised correction. Both happen in every batch.

No public ranking

Tests are for personal progress, not comparison. We never publish marks or rank students against each other.

Stage time built-in

Public speaking and debate practice is part of the regular schedule, not an optional add-on.
