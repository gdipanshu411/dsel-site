Roadmap to Learn English

A clear, step-by-step plan based on advice from top English teachers and language experts (BBC Learning, Cambridge, Oxford, Duolingo). Follow it in order. Don't skip stages.

STAGE 1
Weeks 1–4

Foundation — Sounds & Survival Phrases

Most beginners try to speak before they can hear. Fix that first.

Goals

  • Recognize all 44 English sounds
  • Pronounce vowels and tricky consonants (v/w, th, p/b)
  • Learn 200 most common words
  • Use 50 survival phrases ("Hello", "Thank you", "How are you?", "Excuse me")

Daily plan (30 min)

  • 10 min: Phonetic chart — click and repeat
  • 10 min: Read Dictionary-1 A–E
  • 10 min: Tongue twisters (easy)
STAGE 2
Weeks 5–10

Grammar — The Skeleton

Learn just enough grammar to form correct sentences. Don't try to memorise rules — use them in speech.

Goals

  • Master Present Simple, Past Simple, Future Simple
  • Learn 50 most-used verbs (V1, V2, V3) — see Dictionary-2
  • Use articles (a, an, the) correctly 80% of the time
  • Form basic questions and negatives

Daily plan (45 min)

  • 15 min: Grammar topics (one tense per week)
  • 10 min: 10 new verbs (V1/V2/V3) per day
  • 20 min: Speak in English with a friend or to yourself
STAGE 3
Weeks 11–16

Vocabulary — Build Your Word Bank

You need ~2000 words to handle 90% of daily conversation. Add 5 new words a day.

Goals

  • Active vocabulary of 1000+ words
  • Learn 30 idioms and 30 phrasal verbs
  • Read one short article every day
  • Watch TV with English subtitles (not Hindi subtitles)

Daily plan (60 min)

  • 15 min: Read aloud one news article
  • 15 min: Idioms & phrasal verbs
  • 30 min: Watch English content with English subtitles
STAGE 4
Weeks 17–24

Fluency — Speaking Without Translating

The biggest jump. Stop translating from Hindi in your head. Think in English.

Goals

  • Hold a 5-minute conversation without pausing to translate
  • Speak 5 sentences about any topic instantly
  • Master Present Continuous, Present Perfect, Past Continuous
  • Reduce visible mistakes in 10-sentence speech

Daily plan (75 min)

  • 20 min: Speak with a partner or self-record
  • 20 min: Watch a movie scene + repeat lines (shadowing)
  • 15 min: Write a 100-word diary entry in English
  • 20 min: Practice with our AI Assistant
STAGE 5
Months 7–9

Confidence — Group Discussions & Public Speaking

Speaking 1-on-1 is one thing. Speaking to a group is another. This is where DSEL classes shine.

Goals

  • Lead a 5-minute Group Discussion
  • Give a 3-minute speech on a prepared topic
  • Give a 3-minute speech on an impromptu topic
  • Master interview-style answers (HR rounds)

Daily plan (90 min)

  • 30 min: GD topic — speak both for and against
  • 20 min: Mock interview answers
  • 20 min: Public speaking — record yourself
  • 20 min: Listen back, identify mistakes
STAGE 6
Months 10–12

Mastery — Advanced English

Now you sound natural. Time to polish.

Goals

  • Use slang, chunks, and idioms naturally → see Slang & Gen-Z
  • Use complex tenses (Past Perfect, Future Perfect, Conditionals)
  • Read a full English book / write a 500-word essay
  • Have meaningful debates and discussions

Daily plan (continue ~60 min)

  • 20 min: Read English books, blogs, or articles
  • 20 min: Watch English content WITHOUT subtitles
  • 20 min: Speak / write reflectively
FOREVER

Maintenance — Keep It Alive

Language is a use-it-or-lose-it skill. Even after mastery, keep speaking, reading, listening daily.

  • Speak English at least 30 minutes a day
  • Read one article or short story daily
  • Watch one English video daily
  • Keep adding new words to your vocabulary
