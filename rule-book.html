DSEL Rule Book

Speaking rules, classroom rules, and the DSEL learning code. Read once. Practice every day.

Complete institute notes — ~1.4 MB

Speaking Rules

  1. Speak English from day one. Mistakes are part of learning — never wait until "you are ready."
  2. Speak slowly, clearly, and confidently. Speed comes after clarity.
  3. Think in English. Avoid translating from Hindi — start with simple sentences.
  4. Read aloud daily. 10 minutes a day improves pronunciation faster than anything.
  5. Repeat new words 3 times. Once silently, once aloud, once in a sentence.
  6. Use complete sentences. Avoid one-word answers in class.
  7. Listen first, speak second. Watch English videos with subtitles to train your ear.
  8. Don't fear mistakes. Mistakes mean you are trying — and trying means you are learning.
  9. Practice with a partner. Find one classmate to speak only English with for 10 minutes daily.
  10. Record yourself. Once a week, record a 1-minute speech and listen back.

Classroom Rules

  1. Be on time. Reach the classroom 5 minutes before the batch starts.
  2. Carry your notebook and pen every day. No exceptions.
  3. Phones on silent. Use them only when the teacher allows.
  4. Respect every classmate. Beginners are learning. So are you.
  5. Never laugh at someone's mistake. One day, that will be you.
  6. Ask questions. A doubt unasked stays a doubt forever.
  7. Participate in every activity. GD, debate, role-play, public speaking.
  8. Inform if you'll be absent. A simple WhatsApp message is enough.
  9. Maintain cleanliness. Keep the classroom and materials tidy.
  10. No copying. Tests are for measuring your own progress.

Practice-at-Home Rules

  1. Speak English at home. Even 10 minutes a day with a sibling, parent, or friend.
  2. Watch one English video daily. Pick a topic you enjoy — sports, science, films.
  3. Read one news article daily. Read it aloud after the silent reading.
  4. Maintain a vocabulary diary. Write 5 new words every day with their meanings and a sentence each.
  5. Revise your notes. Spend 15 minutes reviewing what you learned in class.
  6. Listen to English songs/podcasts while travelling or doing chores.
  7. Set a weekly speaking goal. "I will speak only English for 30 minutes on Sunday."

Test & Assessment Rules

  1. Tests are for you, not against you. They show your progress, not your worth.
  2. No public ranking. We never compare students publicly.
  3. Speaking-first scoring. Confidence and clarity are weighed before grammar.
  4. Retake any test. If you didn't do well, take it again — no penalty.
  5. One-on-one feedback. Every test ends with a private conversation about what to improve.
  6. Final assessment is comprehensive. It covers everything from speaking to interview prep.

The DSEL Learning Code

  1. Practice over theory. Speaking improves with daily speaking — never with reading rules alone.
  2. Confidence before correctness. Get the words out first; perfection comes with time.
  3. Friendliness above all. The classroom is a safe space. We never embarrass anyone.
  4. One year of support. The course ends, but your access to practice doesn't.
  5. Word of mouth is our only marketing. Your progress is our reputation. Take it seriously.
